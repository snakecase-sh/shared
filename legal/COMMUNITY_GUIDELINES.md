# Community Guidelines

**Last Updated: December 29, 2025**

## Welcome to snakecase.sh

snakecase.sh is a GitHub-native messaging platform built on the principles of open collaboration, respect, and inclusivity. These Community Guidelines help create a safe, welcoming, and productive environment for all users.

By using snakecase.sh, you agree to abide by these Community Guidelines. Violations may result in warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent account termination.

## Our Values

We are committed to fostering a community that is:

- **Respectful:** Treating all users with dignity and respect
- **Inclusive:** Welcoming people of all backgrounds, identities, and experience levels
- **Collaborative:** Supporting constructive dialogue and teamwork
- **Safe:** Protecting users from harassment, abuse, and harmful content
- **Transparent:** Maintaining clear and fair moderation practices

## 1. Respectful Communication

### 1.1 Be Kind and Courteous

- Treat others as you would like to be treated
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect different viewpoints and experiences
- Be patient with users who may be less experienced
- Assume good intentions and give others the benefit of the doubt

### 1.2 Constructive Feedback

- Provide feedback that is helpful and constructive
- Focus on ideas and content, not personal attacks
- Acknowledge good work and contributions
- Be open to receiving feedback gracefully

### 1.3 Professional Conduct

- Maintain professionalism in all interactions
- Avoid inflammatory or provocative language
- Keep discussions on topic and relevant
- Respect boundaries and personal space

## 2. Prohibited Harassment

### 2.1 Zero Tolerance for Harassment

We have zero tolerance for harassment of any kind. Harassment includes but is not limited to:

- **Personal Attacks:** Insulting, demeaning, or attacking other users
- **Threats:** Threatening violence, harm, or other negative consequences
- **Intimidation:** Deliberately intimidating or bullying other users
- **Stalking:** Unwanted attention, following, or monitoring of users
- **Doxxing:** Sharing private personal information without consent
- **Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, comments, or content
- **Discriminatory Harassment:** Harassment based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, or other protected characteristics

### 2.2 Hate Speech and Discrimination

Absolutely prohibited:

- Hate speech targeting individuals or groups
- Content promoting discrimination or prejudice
- Slurs, epithets, or derogatory language
- Symbols or imagery associated with hate groups
- Content denying or glorifying violence against protected groups

### 2.3 Creating a Safe Environment

- Report harassment when you witness it
- Support users who experience harassment
- Do not engage with or amplify harassing content
- Block users who harass you

## 3. No Spam or Unwanted Content

### 3.1 Spam Prohibited

Do not post:

- **Repetitive Messages:** Sending the same or similar messages repeatedly
- **Unsolicited Advertising:** Promoting products, services, or websites without permission
- **Phishing:** Attempting to obtain sensitive information through deceptive means
- **Malicious Links:** Links to malware, viruses, or harmful websites
- **Bot Activity:** Automated posting or messaging without disclosure
- **Chain Messages:** Forwarding chain letters or pyramid schemes

### 3.2 Relevant and Valuable Content

- Keep messages relevant to the conversation
- Add value to discussions
- Avoid off-topic tangents
- Respect users' time and attention

### 3.3 Self-Promotion Guidelines

- Self-promotion is allowed in moderation
- Clearly disclose your affiliation
- Ensure your content adds value to the community
- Do not spam promotional content

## 4. No Illegal or Harmful Content

### 4.1 Illegal Content Strictly Prohibited

Do not post or share:

- **Illegal Activities:** Content promoting or facilitating illegal activities
- **Child Exploitation:** Any content involving minors in sexual or exploitative contexts
- **Violence:** Content depicting graphic violence or promoting violent acts
- **Terrorism:** Content supporting or promoting terrorist organizations
- **Drugs:** Content promoting illegal drug use or sales
- **Weapons:** Illegal weapons sales or instructions for creating dangerous weapons
- **Fraud:** Scams, fraud, or deceptive practices
- **Intellectual Property Violations:** Pirated content or copyright infringement

### 4.2 Harmful Content

Do not post:

- **Self-Harm:** Content promoting self-harm or suicide
- **Eating Disorders:** Content promoting eating disorders
- **Dangerous Challenges:** Content promoting dangerous activities or challenges
- **False Information:** Deliberately spreading misinformation that could cause harm
- **Graphic Content:** Excessively graphic or disturbing content without appropriate warnings

### 4.3 Privacy Violations

- Do not share others' private information without consent
- Respect confidentiality and non-disclosure agreements
- Do not post screenshots of private conversations without permission
- Protect sensitive personal and professional information

## 5. Intellectual Property and Copyright

### 5.1 Respect Copyright

- Do not share copyrighted material without permission
- Give proper attribution to original creators
- Use only content you have the right to share
- Respect Creative Commons and other licenses

### 5.2 Plagiarism

- Do not claim others' work as your own
- Properly cite sources and references
- Give credit where credit is due

### 5.3 Trademark and Branding

- Do not impersonate other users, organizations, or brands
- Do not use others' trademarks without authorization
- Respect brand guidelines and intellectual property

## 6. Account Integrity

### 6.1 Authentic Identity

- Use your real GitHub account
- Do not create fake or misleading accounts
- Do not impersonate others
- Maintain one primary account

### 6.2 Account Security

- Protect your account credentials
- Do not share your account with others
- Report compromised accounts immediately
- Enable two-factor authentication

### 6.3 Circumventing Restrictions

- Do not create new accounts to evade bans or restrictions
- Do not use technical methods to bypass platform limitations
- Do not manipulate platform features for unintended purposes

## 7. Reporting Mechanisms

### 7.1 How to Report Violations

If you witness or experience violations of these Community Guidelines:

**In-App Reporting:**
- Use the "Report" button on messages or user profiles
- Provide detailed information about the violation
- Include relevant screenshots or evidence

**Email Reporting:**
- Send reports to: abuse@snakecase.sh
- Include your username, the violator's username, and description of the issue
- Attach any supporting evidence

**Urgent Safety Issues:**
- For immediate safety concerns, contact: safety@snakecase.sh
- For security vulnerabilities, contact: security@snakecase.sh

### 7.2 What to Include in Reports

When reporting violations, please provide:

- Specific description of the violation
- When and where it occurred
- Usernames of involved parties
- Screenshots or message links (if available)
- Any additional relevant context

### 7.3 Confidentiality

- Reports are treated confidentially
- We do not disclose reporter identities without consent
- We may contact you for additional information
- You will receive updates on your report when possible

### 7.4 False Reports

- Do not file false or malicious reports
- Do not abuse the reporting system
- False reporting may result in account restrictions

## 8. Enforcement and Consequences

### 8.1 Our Approach to Moderation

We take a fair and transparent approach to moderation:

- **Education First:** We may issue warnings for first-time or minor violations
- **Context Matters:** We consider context and intent when evaluating violations
- **Proportional Response:** Consequences are proportional to the severity of violations
- **Appeals Process:** Users can appeal moderation decisions

### 8.2 Warning System

For minor or first-time violations:

- **First Warning:** Formal notification of guideline violation
- **Second Warning:** Additional notification with reminder of potential consequences
- **Third Warning:** May result in temporary restrictions or account review

### 8.3 Jail (Temporary Restriction)

For moderate violations or repeated minor violations:

- **Temporary Suspension:** Account access temporarily restricted
- **Duration:** Typically 24 hours to 30 days depending on severity
- **Limited Access:** May include read-only access or messaging restrictions
- **Notification:** Users are notified of jail status and duration

### 8.4 Ban (Permanent Termination)

For severe or repeated violations:

- **Permanent Account Termination:** Account is permanently disabled
- **Access Removal:** All access to the platform is revoked
- **Content Removal:** Violating content may be removed
- **No Refunds:** No refunds for any paid services (if applicable)

### 8.5 Violations Resulting in Immediate Ban

The following violations typically result in immediate permanent bans:

- Sharing child exploitation material
- Credible threats of violence
- Doxxing or sharing private information maliciously
- Operating coordinated harassment campaigns
- Evading previous bans
- Severe or repeated violations after multiple warnings

### 8.6 Appeals Process

If you believe a moderation decision was made in error:

**How to Appeal:**
- Email: appeals@snakecase.sh
- Include your username and ticket number (if provided)
- Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
- Provide any relevant evidence or context

**Appeal Review:**
- Appeals are reviewed by a different moderator
- You will receive a response within 7 business days
- Final decisions are made at our discretion
- Repeated frivolous appeals may be ignored

## 9. Special Situations

### 9.1 Content Warnings

When sharing potentially sensitive content:

- Provide clear content warnings
- Use spoiler tags or collapsed sections
- Give users the choice to engage with the content
- Examples: discussions of trauma, mental health, violence

### 9.2 Political and Religious Discussions

- Political and religious discussions are allowed
- Maintain respectful dialogue
- Avoid inflammatory or divisive language
- Respect diverse viewpoints
- Focus on ideas, not personal attacks

### 9.3 Developer and Technical Discussions

- Share knowledge and help others learn
- Avoid gatekeeping or elitism
- Welcome questions from beginners
- Provide constructive code reviews
- Respect different development approaches

### 9.4 Conflicts and Disagreements

When disagreements arise:

- Stay calm and professional
- Focus on the issue, not the person
- Agree to disagree when necessary
- Disengage if the conversation becomes unproductive
- Report harassment or guideline violations

## 10. Platform-Specific Guidelines

### 10.1 Direct Messages

- Respect boundaries and privacy
- Do not send unsolicited commercial messages
- Report unwanted or harassing messages
- Block users who violate your boundaries

### 10.2 Repository Conversations

- Keep discussions relevant to the repository
- Follow repository-specific guidelines
- Respect maintainer decisions
- Contribute constructively

### 10.3 Public vs. Private Spaces

- Understand the visibility of your communications
- Be mindful of what you share in public spaces
- Respect the privacy of private conversations
- Do not screenshot and share private messages without permission
